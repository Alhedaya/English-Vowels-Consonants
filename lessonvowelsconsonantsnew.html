Vowels and Consonants

Vowels

Vowels are sounds produced without any significant constriction or closure of the airflow in the vocal tract.

In English, there are five primary vowel letters: A, E, I, O, and U.

However, the English language has more vowel sounds than vowel letters due to various combinations and diphthongs.

Vowels are characterized by the absence of any obstruction in the mouth while producing the sound.

The tongue is free to move and does not touch any part of the mouth's interior.

Examples of English vowel sounds:
A ➲ cat
E ➲ bed
I ➲ sit
O ➲ hot
U ➲ cup


Consonants

Consonants are sounds produced with some degree of obstruction or closure of the airflow in the vocal tract.

Consonants are produced by obstructing or partially obstructing the airflow with the lips, tongue, teeth, or palate.

Examples of English consonant sounds:

B ➲ bat
H ➲ hat
N ➲ nest
T ➲ top
C ➲ cat
J ➲ jam
P ➲ pen
V ➲ van
D ➲ dog
K ➲ kite
Q ➲ queen
W ➲ wet
F ➲ fish
L ➲ lamp
R ➲ rat
Y ➲ yes
G ➲ goat
M ➲ moon
S ➲ sun
Z ➲ zoo
